# The Sabdia Journal — Writing Playbook

*For Naomi and Tamsin. How to write a Journal entry that sounds like Sabdia and works hard for the business.*

---

## Where to write

`/admin` → **Journal** → **+ New Entry**. Fill the friendly fields, press **Save**.
An entry is invisible to the public until you tick **Published**. Use **Insert starter outline**
in the editor for the house structure, then replace each section with your words.

## The house structure (already in the starter outline)

1. **Hook** — open with the moment that matters. Two or three sentences that earn the read.
2. **The challenge** — what made this hard, interesting, or worth writing about. Be specific.
3. **Sabdia's approach** — how we think: design-first, built in-house, no shortcuts.
4. **Proof** — a real residence that shows it (CASPIAN, QASR, a Collection home). Link it.
5. **Pull quote** — one line you want remembered (client words are gold).
6. **Call to action** — invite the conversation, link `/contact/`.

## Voice — editorial, confident, understated

- Write like the coffee-table book, not the brochure. Short declarative sentences. No exclamation marks.
- **Confidence without boasting**: "One hundred residences taught us…" not "We're the best builder in Brisbane!"
- Specifics are luxury: name the suburb, the stone, the ceiling height. Vague praise is cheap.
- Australian English always: *colour, organise, enquiry, metres*.
- Never invent facts, awards, or client quotes. If you don't know it, leave it out or ask.

## Length & rhythm

- **Sweet spot: 500–800 words** (a 3–4 minute read). Under 300 feels thin; over 1,200 loses people.
- One idea per paragraph, 2–4 sentences each. A `## subheading` every 150–250 words.
- One pull quote per post, maximum.

## Formatting cheatsheet (the Body field)

| You type | Readers see |
|---|---|
| blank line between paragraphs | new paragraph |
| `## Heading` | large section heading |
| `### Heading` | smaller heading |
| `> A line of text` | italic pull quote with a gold rule |
| `- item` (one per line) | bulleted list |
| `**words**` / `*words*` | **bold** / *italic* |
| `[see CASPIAN](/properties/caspian/)` | a link |

## The SEO checklist (30 seconds, every post)

- [ ] **Title** contains something a buyer might search ("knockdown rebuild Ascot", "luxury home builder Brisbane").
- [ ] **Excerpt** is 1–2 sentences that would make *you* click — it's also the Google description.
- [ ] **Hero image** set, with **alt text** describing the photo.
- [ ] **Category** set (keep to a small set: *New Release, Behind the Build, Design Notes, Completed Homes, Buying & Building*).
- [ ] **Web address** is short and readable: `inside-caspian-ascot` not `blog-post-7`.
- [ ] Link at least one **property page** and end with the **contact** link.

## Five ready-to-write headlines

1. **"Inside CASPIAN: how a sloping Ascot block became a five-bedroom sanctuary"** — *Behind the Build.* Walk the design decisions; end at the enquiry form.
2. **"Sold before completion: why Brisbane buyers commit to a Sabdia home off the plan"** — *Completed Homes.* AETHER and the trust story; no invented numbers.
3. **"Design · Develop · Construct: what building in-house actually changes"** — *Design Notes.* The one-team argument, told through one detail (joinery, stone, light).
4. **"Five questions to ask before a knockdown rebuild in inner Brisbane"** — *Buying & Building.* Genuinely useful checklist; positions Sabdia as the answer to question five.
5. **"The Collection, revisited: lessons from MILOS to PETRA"** — *Design Notes.* A thread through completed residences; ends at The Collection.

## Publishing rhythm

One good entry a month beats four rushed ones. Pair each post with an EDM
(the Journal and the newsletter share an audience) and an Instagram cut-down.
